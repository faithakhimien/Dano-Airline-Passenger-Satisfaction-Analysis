## Introduction

Dano Airlines, a UK-based airline headquartered in London, recently recorded a passenger satisfaction rate below 50% for the first time. This decline signals growing dissatisfaction among customers and presents a major business concern, as customer satisfaction directly influences loyalty, retention, and profitability.

This project analyzes over 120,000 passenger survey records to identify the major factors contributing to dissatisfaction and provide data-driven recommendations for improving customer satisfaction.


## Key Metrics: 

1. What is the Overall Satisfaction Rate
2. What is the Lowest Rated Service
3. Lowest Satisfaction Segment
4. Most Dissatisfied Travel Type

### Analysis:

### What is Overall Satisfaction Rate
At 43.4%, Dano's satisfaction rate has dropped below 50% for the first time ever. The overall figure is being held up almost entirely by Business class(69.4%) and returning customers (66.6%), while economy passengers, personal travelers, and younger age groups fall dramatically below average.

### What is the Lowest Rated Service
In-flight Wifi (2.73/5) and Online Booking (2.76/5) rank as the two lowest-performing services across the entire survey. In an era where passengers expect seamless digital connectivity, these scores represent a significant competitive disadvantage. 

### Lowest Satisfaction Segment
At just 18.8% satisfaction, Economy class passengers are overwhelmingly dissatisfied, a stark contrast to Business class at 69.4% and even Economy Plus at 24.6%. Since Economy represents the largest passenger volume on most carriers, this gap has an outsized impact on the overall 43.4% satisfaction rate.

### Most Dissatisfied Travel Type
Only 10.1% of personal travelers are satisfied with their Dano experience, compared to 58.4% of business travelers. This reveals that Dano’s current service model is heavily optimized for corporate passengers, while leisure travelers, who may have different expectations around comfort, entertainment, and value, are being systemically underserved.

### Age Group Satisfaction Patterns
Satisfaction peaks in the 46–60 age bracket (57.4%) and falls sharply for the Under-18 group (16.7%) and seniors 60+ (20.8%). The 18–25 cohort also underperforms at 34.7%. This suggests Dano’s strongest alignment is with mid-career business travelers, while younger and older passengers experience misalignment with the airline’s service offerings.

### Returning & First-time Customers Comparison
Returning customers make up 66.6% of all surveyed passengers. While their loyalty is an asset, the overall 43.4% satisfaction rate means that a significant portion of returning customers are still dissatisfied. If this is not addressed, Dano risks converting loyal customers into churned ones, a far more costly outcome than acquiring new passengers.

## Conclusion and Recommendations:

Dano Airlines’ satisfaction crisis is not random, it is concentrated in three clearly identifiable areas - digital infrastructure, Economy class experience, and personal travel service design. The data tells a consistent story: passengers who fly Economy, travel for personal reasons, or belong to younger or older age groups are being left behind by a service model calibrated for business-class frequent flyers.

Closing this gap requires targeted investment in digital upgrades, Economy class experience redesign, and a leisure traveler strategy.
